Greening the Planet, Empowering the Youth

By Giftson Lewis Situma – Youth Eco Lead
July 13, 2025 | Athi River, Kenya

In an era where climate change threatens ecosystems, livelihoods, and future generations, I believe that environmental action must go hand in hand with youth empowerment. As a passionate environmentalist and youth leader, I have witnessed how environmental initiatives can create powerful opportunities for young people—not just to save the planet, but to build meaningful careers and lives.

Our latest tree planting campaign in Athi River is more than a greening effort. It’s a model of community-driven climate action powered by youth. In partnership with schools, local leaders, and grassroots organizations, we planted over 3,000 indigenous trees in just two months. But more than the numbers, it was the people behind the effort—young volunteers, organizers, and eco-preneurs—that made the real difference.

We trained youths in sustainable agroforestry, seedling propagation, and environmental communication. Some of them, who were previously unemployed or idle, now run small nurseries supplying seedlings for future campaigns. Others have taken up roles in eco-tourism, plastic collection and recycling, and even as social media environmental influencers.

This intersection between green action and youth employment is what inspires me daily. Climate justice is not just about saving trees or oceans. It’s about giving youth purpose, skills, and the chance to lead.

As the founder of Youth Eco Lead, I am committed to building an ecosystem where young people are at the center of environmental restoration and innovation. We envision a future where “green jobs” are not just buzzwords, but accessible, inclusive realities for youths in informal settlements, rural areas, and cities alike.

Our journey has just begun. With every sapling planted, every eco-training hosted, and every partnership formed, we are building both a healthier planet and a generation of empowered, employed, and eco-conscious youth.

Together, we can green the world and grow futures.
